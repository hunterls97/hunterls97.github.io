My name is Hunter Schofield, and I am studying space engineering at York University, Canada.

I grew up in the greater Toronto area and have always had a strong interest in maths and science. One of my favourite pass-times is programming, whether its game development or website creation, coding has always been a passion of mine.

I don’t believe that there is a defined single thing that is unique about me, rather, what makes me unique is the combination of feelings, interests, experiences I have, as well as the hobbies and activities I participate in.

I am currently employed at the regional municipality of York as a special project assistant where I get to combine my passion of programming with my experiences from university.

My Interests

Martial Arts

Ever since I was young, one of my greatest interests has been martial arts

I started studying Aikido when I was fourteen and found that it was a great way to teach myself discipline. This taught discipline has been a great life skill as it gives me a driving force to carry out any projects I wish to complete.

This image is from 2013 when I recieved my orange belt (5th kyu) in Aikido. This image serves as a reminder to myself, telling me that keeping disciplined will see me to greater success in my future.

Guitar

When I was ten, I started playing the guitar as one of my hobbies.

As an engineering student and a front end developer, most of my day is spent thinking about logic problems, doing mathematical computations, etc. However, when I play guitar, I don't need to think much, I only need to play.

Playing guitar gives me the artistic outlet I need when I'm confined to critical thinking all day when I'm at work or at school.

My Goals

Spanish

One of my short-term goals is to learn Spanish. I started learning Spanish in January 2017 on my own and hope to become fluent one day. To reach this goal, I try to study half an hour a day, completing listening, speaking, and writing exercises. I believe one of the best ways to learn something is to leave your comfort zone. In learning Spanish, I leave my comfort zone by speaking with native speakers whenever I can. Even when I feel my Spanish is not enough to hold a full conversation, I try to push myself to communicate however I can.

C/C++

Another goal I hope to accomplish in the near future is to learn the C and C++ languages. I am already familiar with C/C++ syntax, however, I do not have a good foundation with these languages, as I have never created a project using them. As a developer, I believe it is important to keep learning and expanding your skillset so that you have more tools available to develop solutions with. Once I learn these programming languages, it is very likely that I will continue developing my knowledge by learning others.

5 Year Plan

My long-term goals have been incorporated in what I like to call my '5-year plan'. Over the next five years, my primary goal is to complete my undergraduate program in space engineering, and get accepted into a master’s program related to space data systems and operating systems. To do this, I will need to achieve a number of smaller goals that will mature my skillset and aid in achieving my overarching goal. The two goals displayed above are the ones I am currently working towards. Over the next 5 years, I plan to developing my own Linux kernel, achieve my black belt in Aikido, and publish my own Unity 3D game. I believe these goals will come together and assist me in acquiring my undergraduate degree, and gain admission into a master’s program.
